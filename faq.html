Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is breast cancer?

    It is a disease where abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the breast tissue.

  • Who is at risk for breast cancer?

    Risk increases with age, family history, certain gene mutations, and lifestyle factors.

  • What are the symptoms of breast cancer?

    Lump in the breast, change in shape, nipple discharge, or skin dimpling.

  • How often should I perform a self-exam?

    Once a month, ideally a few days after your period ends.

  • Is breast cancer hereditary?

    Yes, in some cases due to mutations in genes like BRCA1 and BRCA2.

  • What age should I start screening?

    Routine mammograms are recommended starting at age 40 or earlier if high-risk.

  • Can men get breast cancer?

    Yes, although it is rare, men can develop breast cancer too.

  • What lifestyle changes help prevention?

    Healthy diet, regular exercise, maintaining weight, and limiting alcohol.

  • What are mammograms?

    X-ray images of the breast used to detect tumors or abnormalities early.

  • Where can I get help?

    You can visit your local health center or talk to a healthcare provider for guidance.
